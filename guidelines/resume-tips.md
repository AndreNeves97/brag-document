## Lenght

A concise, targeted resume shows recruiters your ability to synthesize, prioritize, and convey your most important achievements. Top senior-level candidates have resumes between 450 and 850 words long (~1-2 pages).

## Usage of numbers and metrics

You should add hard numbers into your resume.

Recruiters are looking for evidence of impact, and hard numbers help with this. To explain this,
let's compare these two lines:

- Weaker: In charge of creating new processes and improving communication between departments.
- Stronger: Managed a process re-engineering project to improve and consolidate end-to-end service processes; restructured communication flow among 10 departments and cut down paperwork by 75%.

Notice how using hard numbers emphasizes the impact of your work â€” this is what
recruiters look for on your resume. Let's now work through your own resume...


## Remove Weak Words

You should remove weak action verbs from your resume.

Avoid words like: Work With, Responsible For, Contributed, Helped, Worked, Responsible, Was, Assisted and Worked With.

You should replace these words with stronger alternatives.

Instead of:
- Assisted, use Facilitated, Co-ordinated, or Influenced
- Contributed, use Facilitated, Co-ordinated, or Influenced
- Helped, use Facilitated, Counselled, Influenced, or Mediated
- Responsible, use Preparing, Developing, or Implementing
- Responsible For, use Pioneered, Established, or Initiated
- Was, use Developed, Prepared, or Improved
- Work With, use Liaise with, Advise, or Persuade
- Worked, use Prepared, Developed, or Implemented
- Worked With, use Liaised, Advised, Addressed, or Persuaded


## Use of bullets

Though, there might be a couple of lines in paragraph form that you might want to change to
shorter bullet points.

Here's an example from your resume: [Company Name] is an EdTech that is focused to make education accessible to all people, through business partners, such as internet providers, who pay to provide free access to the app for their customers. Its main product is the [Product Name], available on Play Store, App Store, and as Web App.

Recruiters may find a long line like this hard to read, so consider splitting it up into multiple bullet points to help your achievements stand out.

That said, it's fine to have 1 or 2 short paragraphs on your resume â€” just make sure they're no more than 2-3 lines long.


## Filler words

You should remove unnecessary filler words and adverbs from your resume. These words just take up space and can often be replaced by more specific examples (e.g. numbers/metrics), or removed from your resume.

Filler words used: some, several, in addition to.




## Remove personal pronouns

It is a standard in resumes to omit personal pronouns. You should remove from your resume personal pronouns like: I, my, Its, It, their, it, me and them.

- Before: I managed a 5-member cross-functional (product, engineering, sales, support) team and we coordinated with my companyâ€™s six business partners toward the successful launch of our ecommerce platform.

- After: Managed a 5-member cross-functional (product, engineering, sales, support) team and coordinated with six business partners toward the successful launch of an e-commerce platform.



## Remove passive voice

Consider rewriting passive voice sentences to the active voice, which not only increases your resume's impact but also makes it clearer and more concise.

Passive voice examples:
- Customer satisfaction was improved by 25%
- 25% increase in customers was achieved by the company with new marketing strategy
- New manufacturing processes was implemented for ABC company

Active voice examples:
- Improved customer satisfaction by 25%
- Increased customers by 25% by implementing new marketing strategy
- Implemented new manufacturing processes for ABC company

In more technical terms, the active voice describes a sentence where the subject performs the action stated by the verb. In passive voice sentences, the subject is acted upon by the verb.
